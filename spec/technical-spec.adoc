Any lens or frame rim that implements this specification and passes the tests herein may claim UES compliance.

== Introduction

=== Purpose

This specification exists to enable a market in interchangeable prescription lenses.
When every compliant lens and every compliant frame rim implement this mechanical interface, a consumer can:

* own multiple frames in one size and swap lenses between them at home without tools;
* replace a broken frame without discarding functional lenses;
* order lenses online and receive them ready to self-install.

=== Scope

This specification normatively defines:

. The lens contour model - a UES lens outline as a list of control points of a cubic Bézier curve.
. Size codes (XS through L) that scale the unit shape to the physical lens diameter, together with the bridge widths paired with each size.
. The bevel ridge on the lens edge and the matching bevel groove on the frame rim inner wall — the single mechanical feature that makes the standard work.
. Lens edge geometry constraints (edge zone width, minimum edge thickness in the bevel zone).
. Frame rim body geometry (rim channel width and depth, aperture shape).
. Material requirements for the bevel zone of both product types.
. Surface finish requirements in the bevel zone.
. Environmental and durability requirements (temperature, humidity, UV, insertion/removal cycle life).
. Dimensional tolerances on all normative dimensions.
. Conformance test procedures (self-tested; no external laboratory required).
. Required marking on conforming products.

The following are outside scope:

* Optical prescription values, coatings, tints, and UV absorption — these remain entirely at the discretion of the lens manufacturer.
* Frame materials, temple styles, nose pad design, hinge mechanisms, and all other frame geometry not at the rim aperture.

=== Terms and Definitions

Bevel groove:: The V-shaped groove machined or moulded continuously around the inner (aperture) wall of a UES-compliant frame rim; the female mating feature of the interface.

Bevel ridge:: The V-shaped ridge formed continuously around the full perimeter of the edge of a UES-compliant lens; the male mating feature of the interface.

Bevel zone:: The annular band at the perimeter of the lens edge that carries the bevel ridge, bounded by the front face and rear face of the lens; also, the corresponding annular region of the frame rim inner wall that carries the bevel groove.

Bridge width:: The clear distance between the inner nasal edges of the two lens rims, measured horizontally at the datum plane defined in ISO 8624 §5.

Clearance fit:: An assembly condition in which the groove dimensions exceed the ridge dimensions by a defined amount, ensuring the lens can be inserted and removed by hand.

Compliant lens:: A lens that satisfies all normative requirements of this specification for the lens role.

Compliant frame rim:: A frame rim that satisfies all normative requirements of this specification for the frame rim role.

Datum plane:: The vertical plane parallel to the lens front surface, passing through the horizontal centreline of the lens, as defined in ISO 8624.

Edge zone:: The annular region of the lens extending 3.0 mm radially inward from the outer edge of the lens body; the region in which the bevel zone is located.

Fully seated:: The condition in which the bevel ridge apex contacts the bevel groove apex along the entire perimeter and the lens front surface is flush with the frame rim front face, within the tolerances of this specification.

Insertion force:: The maximum force required to press a compliant lens into a fully seated position in a compliant rim.

Nominal:: The target dimension to which tolerances apply.

Retention force:: The minimum force required to unseat a fully seated compliant lens from a compliant rim by applying a load perpendicular to the lens plane at the centre of the lens.

Rim channel:: The annular recess in the frame rim that receives the lens edge including the bevel ridge; the bevel groove is cut into the inner wall of this channel.

Size code:: A one or two letter identifier (e.g. "XS") of the lens diameter.

Shape code:: A one or two letter identifier (e.g. "C") of the lens contour shape.

Product code:: A designator of the form UES-TT-SS, where TT is a one- or two-letter shape-type identifier (e.g., C for Circular) and SS is a size code (e.g., XS, S, M, L), that uniquely identifies a lens shape and physical scale; two products are mechanically compatible if and only if they carry the same product code.

Control point:: One of the ordered points P₀, P₁, … that define a UES looped cubic Bézier contour; see <<_lens_contour_model>>.

Unit shape:: A lens contour expressed in normalized coordinates such that (a) the shape centroid is at the origin and (b) the maximum distance between any two points on the lens contour (the shape diameter) is exactly 1.

== System Overview

=== Role of the UES Interface

[informative]
The UES interface is the sole point of mechanical coupling between a lens and a frame rim.
It is intentionally defined as a single, simple V-bevel feature so that:

* lenses and rims can be manufactured by independent parties without coordination beyond this specification;
* installation requires no tools, no adhesive, and no fasteners;
* the interface self-centres the lens during installation by the geometry of the V-groove.

=== Product Types

This specification governs two product types:

*Lenses* — optical elements carrying a bevel ridge.
A lens may carry a single-vision, progressive, bifocal, or any other prescription optic; it may also be plain (plano).
The lens body material, optical coatings, tints, and all optical properties beyond those defined in this specification are at the manufacturer's discretion.

*Frame rims* — the structural elements of a spectacle frame that form the apertures carrying lenses.
A frame rim carries a bevel groove.
The frame body material, temple mechanism, nose pads, and all other frame properties are at the manufacturer's discretion.

=== Interchangeability Model

A compliant lens of product code UES-TT-SS SHALL mate with any compliant frame rim of the same product code.
A compliant lens of product code UES-TT-SS SHALL NOT be expected to mate with a frame rim of a different product code.

[[_lens_contour_model]]
== Lens Contour Model

=== Overview

All UES lens outline shapes are defined as *looped cubic Bézier curves* in a normalized coordinate system.
This decouples shape geometry from physical size: the shape is specified once as a unit shape with a fixed normalized diameter of 1, and the size codes in <<_size_catalogue>> scale it by the lens diameter to produce physical dimensions.

=== Coordinate System and Normalization

The coordinate system for unit shapes is:

* Origin at the area centroid of the lens contour (the centroid of the region bounded by the looped cubic Bézier curve).
* X-axis: horizontal, positive toward the temporal (right) side.
* Y-axis: vertical, positive toward the superior (top) direction.

A *unit shape* is a lens contour expressed in normalized coordinates such that:

* The area centroid of the contour lies at the origin; and
* The *shape diameter* - the maximum distance between any two points on the lens contour - is exactly 1.

Formally, for the unit-shape curve C(t), t ∈ [0, 1]:

  max_{t,s ∈ [0,1]} ‖C(t) - C(s)‖ = 1.

A shape code identifies a particular unit shape via an ordered list of control points in this normalized coordinate system.

.UES lens contour coordinate system and normalization
image::diagrams/definition.drawio.png[UES lens contour coordinate system and normalization,600,align="center"]

=== Looped Cubic Bézier Representation

A UES lens contour is defined by an ordered list of M = 3N control points P₀, P₁, …, P_{M−1}, forming N cubic Bézier segments.

Segment i (i = 0 … N−1) is the cubic Bézier curve:

  Bᵢ(t) = (1−t)³ P_{3i}
        + 3(1−t)² t  P_{3i+1}
        + 3(1−t) t²  P_{3i+2}
        + t³         P_{(3i+3) mod M},   t ∈ [0, 1]

The control point sequence SHALL be ordered *counter-clockwise* when viewed from the front of the lens (positive-Y-superior convention).

[informative]
In a cubic Bézier segment, P_{3i} and P_{(3i+3) mod M} are the on-curve endpoints (the curve passes through them), and P_{3i+1} and P_{3i+2} are the off-curve handle points that shape the segment without being on the curve.

*Looped:* The contour is closed.
The last segment (i = N−1) ends at P_{(3N) mod M} = P₀ — no repeated point is stored; the closure is implicit in the modular indexing.

=== Physical Realisation

To realise a unit shape at physical size, multiply every control point coordinate by the lens diameter *d* (mm):

  (x_phys, y_phys) = (d · x_unit, d · y_unit).

Because the unit shape has contour diameter 1 by definition, the physical contour diameter equals *d*.

[[shape-catalogue]]
=== Shape Catalogue

Each shape in the catalogue is defined by its shape code, a brief description, and an ordered list of M = 3N control points in the normalized coordinate system of <<_coordinate_system_and_normalization>>.
The control points fully determine the unit shape; all other properties (physical dimensions, tolerances) follow from the Physical Realisation rules in <<_physical_realisation>>.

[[shape-C]]
==== Shape C — Circular

[informative]
Shape C is a close approximation of a circle, defined by N = 4 cubic Bézier segments (M = 12 control points).

=== Lens Contour Dimensions

The physical lens contour for a product code is the unit shape (<<shape-catalogue>>) of the declared shape code, scaled uniformly by the lens diameter *d* of the declared size code.
The outermost extent of the flat lens body edge (excluding the bevel ridge) is the *lens diameter* *d*.
The bevel ridge extends beyond this boundary by the bevel depth (1.0 mm), so the overall maximum extent including the ridge is: *d* + 2 × 1.0 mm.
The physical extent in each axis is determined by the unit shape's coordinate range scaled by *d*.

NOTE: Frame rim aperture and rim channel dimensions are defined to accept the lens including the bevel ridge.
See <<_frame_rim_geometry>>.

=== Bridge Width

Bridge width is a property of the frame, not the lens.
A compliant frame rim SHALL have a bridge width within the tolerance of the declared product code.
A compliant lens does not carry a bridge width dimension.

== Mechanical Specification

=== Overview

The interface between a compliant lens and a compliant frame rim consists of:

. A continuous V-shaped bevel ridge running around the full perimeter of the lens edge.
. A matching continuous V-shaped bevel groove in the inner wall of the frame rim.
. A defined clearance between ridge and groove that allows hand insertion and removal while maintaining adequate retention force.

All dimensions in this section are normative.
See <<_dimensional_tolerances>> for the tolerance on each parameter.

=== Lens Body Edge Geometry

==== Edge Zone

The edge zone is the annular band extending 3.0 mm radially inward from the outer boundary of the lens body.
The bevel ridge SHALL be contained entirely within the edge zone.
No optical coating, hardening layer, or printed marking shall extend into the region within 0.5 mm of the outer boundary of the lens body (the lane in which the bevel ridge flank surfaces lie).

==== Minimum Edge Thickness in the Bevel Zone

The lens edge SHALL have a minimum thickness of 2.5 mm in the bevel zone.
This thickness is measured perpendicular to the lens plane at the perimeter, from the front face of the lens to the rear face.

NOTE: High-minus lenses with strong negative power may achieve edge thicknesses well in excess of 2.5 mm.
High-plus lenses with strong positive power may approach the minimum; manufacturers SHALL verify compliance before releasing a lens design.

==== Edge Face Perpendicularity

The outer cylindrical face of the lens edge (the surface on which the bevel ridge is formed) SHALL be perpendicular to the lens plane within 2°.
Deviation is acceptable only if it does not interfere with the bevel groove contact described in <<_bevel_interface>>.

[[_bevel_interface]]
=== Bevel Interface

The bevel ridge (on the lens) and the bevel groove (on the frame rim) together form the single mechanical coupling of the UES interface.

==== Lens Bevel Ridge

A symmetrical V-shaped ridge SHALL run continuously around the full perimeter of the lens edge.
No interruption of more than 0.1 mm arc length is permitted.

The 0.05 mm per-flank clearance produces a nominal air gap that:

* allows hand insertion of a cold lens into a cold rim (no thermal interference);
* accommodates thermal expansion from -20 °C to +60 °C for common lens and rim materials;
* provides predictable contact force along both flanks simultaneously (the V geometry centres the lens axially).

The bevel interface is a clearance fit.

==== Rim Aperture Diameter

The rim aperture diameter (the diameter of the opening that the lens occupies) SHALL equal the nominal lens body diameter of the declared product code within the tolerances of <<_dimensional_tolerances>>.
The rim aperture diameter is measured from the inner edges of the rim channel, i.e., the diameter at the entrance plane of the channel, before the groove.

==== Frame Rim Face Flatness

The front face of the frame rim (the surface visible from the front) SHALL be planar to within 0.30 mm over the aperture diameter.
This planarity ensures that a fully seated lens front face is flush with the rim front face.

[[_materials]]
=== Material Requirements

==== Lens Edge Bevel Zone Material

The material forming the bevel ridge and the edge zone SHALL:

* Have a Shore D hardness ≥ 70 (or equivalent for the material class) to prevent permanent deformation of the ridge under insertion force.
* Have a Charpy unnotched impact strength ≥ 5 kJ/m² (ISO 179-1) so the ridge does not fracture during insertion or removal at temperatures down to -20 °C.
* Not exhibit cold flow (creep) that reduces bevel ridge depth by more than 0.05 mm after 1,000 hours at 60 °C under a lateral force of 5 N.

NOTE: Common lens materials that satisfy these requirements include CR-39 (allyl diglycol carbonate), polycarbonate (PC), Trivex, and high-index glass.
The manufacturer SHALL verify compliance for any material not in this list.

==== Frame Rim Bevel Groove Material

The material forming the bevel groove SHALL:

* Have a Shore D hardness ≥ 60 (lower than lens to place incremental wear preferentially on the rim, which is the less costly element to replace).
* Have sufficient elasticity to allow hand flexing of the rim sufficient to pass the installation test in <<_test_installation>>.
* Not exhibit cold flow that narrows the groove opening width by more than 0.05 mm after 1,000 hours at 60 °C under a lateral force of 5 N.

NOTE: Common frame rim materials that satisfy these requirements include acetate (cellulose acetate), nylon (PA), nylon-glass fibre composite, titanium alloy (for metal rims with grooved wire), and spring steel wire.

==== Prohibited Materials and Treatments

The following SHALL NOT be applied within the bevel zone on either the lens ridge or the groove:

* Soft elastomeric coatings with Shore A hardness < 50.
* Lubricants containing solvents that attack polycarbonate (ketones, esters, chlorinated solvents).
* Adhesives or bonding agents (the interface is mechanical only).

== Environmental and Durability Requirements

=== Temperature

==== Operating Temperature Range

A fully mated lens-and-rim assembly SHALL meet all retention force requirements of <<_retention_force>> throughout the operating temperature range of -20 °C to +60 °C.

==== Storage Temperature Range

A lens or frame rim, un-mated, SHALL survive storage at -30 °C to +80 °C for up to 72 hours with no dimensional change exceeding 0.05 mm on any normative bevel dimension, measured after return to 23 °C ± 2 °C.

==== Thermal Shock

A mated assembly SHALL survive five cycles of:

. Immersion in water at 5 °C ± 2 °C for 30 minutes.
. Transfer within 30 seconds to water at 55 °C ± 2 °C for 30 minutes.

After completion, the assembly SHALL pass the retention force test (<<_test_retention>>) and the lens SHALL show no visible cracks under 1,000 lux illumination.

=== Humidity

A mated assembly SHALL survive 240 hours at 40 °C and 95% relative humidity (non-condensing) with no loss of retention force below the minimum defined in <<_retention_force>> and no visible corrosion, delamination, or warping that would prevent re-seating.

=== Ultraviolet Exposure

A lens and a frame rim SHALL each survive 200 hours of UV exposure per ISO 4892-3 (fluorescent UV lamps, UV-A 340 nm, irradiance 0.76 W/m²·nm) with no:

* Loss of bevel ridge depth exceeding 0.05 mm.
* Loss of groove opening width exceeding 0.05 mm.
* Cracking or crazing of any bevel zone surface.

=== Insertion and Removal Cycle Life

The bevel interface SHALL be rated for a minimum of 500 insertion and removal cycles (one cycle = full installation to fully seated + full removal).

After 500 cycles performed per <<_test_insertion_cycle>>, the assembly SHALL:

. Pass the retention force test.
. Show no bevel ridge depth reduction exceeding 0.10 mm.
. Show no groove opening width increase exceeding 0.10 mm.
. Allow the lens to be installed and removed by hand without tools.

NOTE: 500 cycles is the baseline.
A manufacturer MAY claim a higher cycle rating (e.g., "UES 2000-cycle rated") if the product passes the endurance test at the higher cycle count.

=== Drop and Impact (Informative)

[informative]
UES-compliant lenses and rims are not required to pass a drop or ballistic impact test as part of this specification; such requirements are governed by national eyewear safety standards (e.g., EN ISO 16321, ANSI Z87.1).
Manufacturers are encouraged to test their products to the applicable safety standard independently of UES compliance.

A compliant lens of any product code SHALL seat fully in a compliant rim of the same product code by applying a force no greater than:

|Maximum Insertion Force

|12 N

|UES-C-S
|14 N

|UES-C-M
|16 N

|UES-C-L
|18 N
|===

NOTE: Forces scale with perimeter length.
The limits above correspond to approximately 75 N/m of bevel perimeter.
The insertion force test procedure is defined in <<_test_installation>>.

[[_retention_force]]
=== Retention Force

Retention force is the minimum force required to unseat a fully seated compliant lens by applying a centralised normal load perpendicular to the lens plane.

A fully seated compliant lens SHALL require a force no less than the values below to unseat, measured per <<_test_retention>>:

[cols="1,1", options="header"]
|===
|Product Code
|Minimum Retention Force

|UES-C-XS
|20 N

|UES-C-S
|22 N

|UES-C-M
|25 N

|UES-C-L
|28 N
|===

NOTE: The minimum retention force is set high enough that a lens will not unseat when the frame is dropped face-down from 1 m, but low enough to be overcome by hand during intentional removal.

=== Seating Flush Requirement

When fully seated, the lens front face SHALL be flush with the frame rim front face within ± 0.30 mm across the full rim perimeter.
A lens recessed more than 0.30 mm or proud more than 0.30 mm of the rim front face is non-conforming.

[[_conformance_test_procedures]]
== Conformance Test Procedures

This section defines the normative self-test procedures that a manufacturer SHALL execute on a representative production sample before claiming UES compliance.

Test conditions unless otherwise stated: 23 °C ± 2 °C, 50% ± 5% RH.

Required test equipment:

* Calibrated digital calipers: resolution ≤ 0.01 mm, accuracy ± 0.02 mm.
* Optical comparator or contact profilometer capable of measuring Ra to ± 0.05 µm.
* Force gauge: capacity ≥ 50 N, resolution ≤ 0.5 N, accuracy ± 1%.
* Fixture for retention force test (described in <<_test_retention>>).
* UV test chamber (for UV durability test; per ISO 4892-3).
* Temperature-controlled water baths and oven (for thermal tests).

[[_test_dimensional]]
=== Test 1 — Dimensional Verification

*Purpose:* Confirm that all nominal dimensions are within the tolerances of <<_dimensional_tolerances>>.

*Sample size:* Five lenses and five frame rims per declared product code per production batch.

*Procedure:*

. Condition all samples at 23 °C ± 2 °C for a minimum of 2 hours before measurement.
. Measure the lens body diameter at four orthogonal orientations (0°, 45°, 90°, 135°) using calibrated calipers.
  Record the mean and the max-min spread (roundness deviation).
  Mean SHALL be within ± 0.10 mm of nominal.
  Roundness deviation SHALL not exceed 0.10 mm.
. Measure the bevel ridge depth at 12 equally-spaced positions around the perimeter using an optical comparator or calibrated profilometer cross-section.
  All readings SHALL be within ± 0.05 mm of 1.00 mm.
. Measure the bevel ridge opening width at the same 12 positions.
  All readings SHALL be within ± 0.05 mm of 1.20 mm.
. Measure the bevel ridge included angle at four positions (0°, 90°, 180°, 270°) using the profilometer cross-section.
  All readings SHALL be within ± 1° of 120°.
. Measure the bevel ridge apex radius at four positions.
  All readings SHALL be ≥ 0.15 mm and ≤ 0.25 mm.
. Measure the bevel ridge apex axial position at four positions relative to the geometric mid-plane of the lens edge.
  All readings SHALL be within ± 0.10 mm of the mid-plane.
. Repeat steps 3-7 for the frame rim groove, applying the groove rows of <<_dimensional_tolerances>>.
. Measure the rim aperture diameter at four orientations.
  Mean SHALL be within ± 0.10 mm of nominal.
. Measure the rim channel axial width at four positions.
  All readings SHALL be ≥ nominal lens edge thickness + 0.20 mm.
. Measure bridge width at the datum plane per ISO 8624.
  Result SHALL be within ± 0.20 mm of nominal for the declared product code.

*Pass criterion:* All measurements within the tolerances of <<_dimensional_tolerances>> for all five samples.
Any sample outside tolerance is non-conforming; the batch SHALL be investigated before release.

[[_test_installation]]
=== Test 2 — Insertion Force Test

*Purpose:* Confirm the lens can be fully seated without exceeding the maximum insertion force.

*Sample size:* Three lens-and-rim pairs per product code.

*Procedure:*

. Prepare one compliant lens and one compliant frame rim of the same product code, freshly cleaned (isopropyl alcohol wipe, dry).
  No lubricant.
. Mount the frame rim in the force gauge fixture with the rim aperture facing upward.
. Centre the lens over the aperture, bevel ridge aligned with the groove.
. Apply a steadily increasing perpendicular force to the lens centre using the force gauge, at a rate of 5-10 N/s, until the lens is fully seated (audible or tactile click, or flush seating confirmed).
. Record the peak force during seating.

*Pass criterion:* Peak force SHALL not exceed the value in <<_insertion_force>> for the declared product code for all three pairs.

NOTE: At temperatures below 10 °C, insertion force may increase due to material stiffening.
Manufacturers SHOULD test at the lower end of the operating temperature range if the product is intended for cold-climate use.

[[_test_retention]]
=== Test 3 — Retention Force Test

*Purpose:* Confirm the seated lens resists unseating with at least the minimum retention force.

*Sample size:* Three lens-and-rim pairs per product code (may reuse pairs from Test 2).

*Fixture:* A rigid plate with a circular aperture slightly smaller than the rim outer diameter, mounted horizontally so the rim is clamped against the plate and the lens hangs below.
The force gauge pulls the lens downward from its centre.

*Procedure:*

. Seat the lens fully in the rim per Test 2, then mount the rim rigidly in the retention fixture with the lens facing down.
. Apply a centred perpendicular tensile force (pulling the lens downward, away from the rim) at a rate of 5-10 N/s using the force gauge.
. Record the force at which the lens begins to unseat (first bevel ridge disengagement, detected by a force drop ≥ 10% from peak).

*Pass criterion:* Unseating force SHALL meet or exceed the minimum in <<_retention_force>> for all three pairs.

[[_test_continuity]]
=== Test 4 — Bevel Continuity Inspection

*Purpose:* Confirm the bevel ridge and bevel groove are continuous around the full perimeter.

*Sample size:* 100% inspection in production; 3 samples per product code for initial qualification.

*Procedure:*

. Under bright directional illumination (minimum 1,000 lux), visually inspect the full perimeter of the bevel ridge on the lens for gaps, chips, tool marks, or discontinuities.
. Measure any suspected gap with a calibrated optical comparator.
  Any single gap > 0.10 mm arc length is a non-conformance.
. Repeat for the bevel groove on the frame rim.

*Pass criterion:* No gap > 0.10 mm arc length on any inspected surface.

=== Test 5 — Seating Flush Check

*Purpose:* Confirm the lens front face is flush with the rim front face when fully seated.

*Sample size:* Three lens-and-rim pairs per product code.

*Procedure:*

. Seat the lens fully in the rim.
. Using a depth gauge with a bridging foot, or a straightedge with feeler gauges, measure the step height between the lens front face and the rim front face at 12 equally-spaced positions around the perimeter.
. Record the maximum positive value (lens proud of rim) and the maximum negative value (lens recessed relative to rim).

*Pass criterion:* All readings SHALL be in the range -0.30 mm to +0.30 mm.

[[_test_insertion_cycle]]
=== Test 6 — Insertion/Removal Cycle Endurance

*Purpose:* Confirm the bevel interface lasts at least 500 insertion/removal cycles.

*Sample size:* One lens-and-rim pair per product code.

*Procedure:*

. Execute 500 complete insertion/removal cycles.
  One cycle = seat the lens fully per Test 2, then remove by hand without tools (lever from the nasal or temporal edge with a fingernail if necessary).
  Rate: minimum 1 cycle per minute; maximum 10 cycles per minute.
. After 250 cycles, perform the bevel dimensional checks of Test 1 (steps 3-8 only) and record measurements.
. After 500 cycles, perform Test 1 (full), Test 2 (insertion force), Test 3 (retention force), and Test 5 (flush seating).

*Pass criterion:*

* No bevel ridge depth reduction > 0.10 mm from initial measured value.
* No groove opening width increase > 0.10 mm from initial measured value.
* Retention force ≥ minimum from <<_retention_force>>.
* Insertion force ≤ maximum from <<_insertion_force>>.
* Lens can be installed and removed by hand without tools.

=== Test 7 — Thermal Shock

*Purpose:* Confirm the assembly survives temperature cycling without loss of retention.

*Sample size:* One lens-and-rim pair per product code.

*Procedure:*

. Seat the lens in the rim.
. Perform five cycles:
  .. Submerge in 5 °C ± 2 °C water for 30 minutes.
  .. Transfer within 30 seconds to 55 °C ± 2 °C water for 30 minutes.
. Remove; dry at 23 °C for 1 hour.
. Perform Test 3 (retention force) and visually inspect the lens for cracks under 1,000 lux illumination.

*Pass criterion:* Retention force ≥ minimum.
No visible cracks on the lens.

=== Test 8 — Humidity Exposure

*Purpose:* Confirm the assembly survives prolonged high humidity.

*Sample size:* One lens-and-rim pair per product code.

*Procedure:*

. Seat the lens in the rim.
. Place the mated assembly in a humidity chamber at 40 °C ± 2 °C, 95% ± 2% RH for 240 hours.
. Remove; condition at 23 °C for 2 hours.
. Perform Test 3 (retention force) and visually inspect for corrosion or delamination.
  Confirm the lens can be re-seated and removed by hand at least once.

*Pass criterion:* Retention force ≥ minimum.
No visible corrosion that would prevent re-seating.

=== Test 9 — UV Durability

*Purpose:* Confirm the bevel zone survives UV exposure without dimensional degradation.

*Sample size:* One lens and one rim per product code, un-mated.

*Procedure:*

. Measure baseline bevel ridge depth (lens) and groove opening width (rim) per Test 1 at four positions.
. Expose both specimens to 200 hours UV per ISO 4892-3 (UV-A 340 nm, 0.76 W/m²·nm).
. Condition at 23 °C for 2 hours.
. Repeat dimensional measurements at the same four positions.

*Pass criterion:*

* No bevel ridge depth reduction > 0.05 mm.
* No groove opening width change > 0.05 mm.
* No cracking or crazing of any bevel zone surface visible under 10x magnification.

[[_marking_and_identification]]
== Marking and Identification

=== Required Marking — Lenses

Every UES-compliant lens SHALL carry the following marking, laser-etched, silk-screened, pad-printed, or applied via a durable label:

. The UES product code (e.g., *UES-C-M*), positioned in the edge zone on the nasal side of the lens.
. The manufacturer's name or registered trademark.
. The applicable specification version.

The marking SHALL be legible under 10x magnification.
It SHALL be positioned within the edge zone and SHALL NOT overlap the bevel ridge.
It SHALL survive Test 8 (humidity) without becoming illegible.

=== Required Marking — Frames

Every UES-compliant frame SHALL carry the following on the frame body:

. The UES product code (e.g., *UES-C-M*).
. The bridge width in mm (e.g., *19 mm*) if not deducible from the product code alone.
. The manufacturer's name or registered trademark.
. The applicable specification version.

The marking may be on a permanent label on the inner temple, on the nose bridge, or laser-etched on the rim body.

=== UES Logo Use

The UES-IF logo and associated trademarks may only be applied to a product by organisations that:

. Are a UES-IF Full Member or Logo Licensee in good standing.
. Have performed and documented the conformance tests of <<_conformance_test_procedures>> for the specific product line.

Logo use is governed by the UES-IF Trademark/Logo Licence Agreement, a separate document from this specification.

NOTE: The technical specification is free to implement without fees or registration.
Logo rights require a separate agreement with UES-IF.

[[_compliance_declaration]]
== Compliance Declaration

=== Declaring Compliance

A manufacturer declares a product UES-compliant by:

. Executing all applicable conformance tests from <<_conformance_test_procedures>> on a representative sample of the product.
. Completing and retaining a Conformance Test Record (CTR) as defined in <<_ctr>>.
. Applying the required marking from <<_marking_and_identification>> to the product.

No submission to UES-IF is required to declare compliance.
No external laboratory is required.

=== Re-testing Requirement

Re-testing is required when any of the following changes are made to a compliant product:

. Change in bevel zone material or material supplier.
. Change in bevel ridge or groove geometry (tooling change).
. Change in lens body diameter by more than 0.05 mm.
. Change in surface finish process in the bevel zone.

A cosmetic change (colour, coating tint, lens design outside the bevel zone, frame decoration) does NOT require re-testing.

[appendix]
== Appendix A — Informative Design Guidelines

=== A.1 Lens Edge Preparation

[informative]
The bevel ridge is most economically produced by a dedicated bevelling wheel on a CNC lens edging machine, set to a V-groove profile matching the ridge geometry.
The operator enters:

* V-depth: 1.0 mm
* V-width at base: 1.2 mm
* V-angle: 120°
* Apex axial offset: (edge thickness / 2) to centre the ridge on the mid-plane

It is recommended that the bevel is cut as the final step after all other edging operations to avoid chipping the apex during subsequent operations.

=== A.2 Frame Rim Groove Production

[informative]
For injection-moulded plastic frames, the bevel groove is most reliably incorporated into the mould cavity.
For metal frames using a groove wire (titanium or steel), the wire cross-section should be designed to the groove geometry and the wire welded or adhesively bonded to the rim channel floor.

Groove geometry geometry check: with a 1.30 mm opening and 120° included angle, the theoretical groove depth from the opening to the apex is:

  depth = (opening width / 2) / tan(60°) = 0.65 / 1.732 ≈ 0.375 mm

Adding the 0.05 mm apex clearance gives 0.425 mm to the groove apex from the rim wall surface.
However, the full groove depth in this specification is 1.05 mm measured from the rim inner wall surface, because the groove is cut into the wall, not just the surface.
Manufacturers should model the groove cross-section carefully in CAD and verify against a produced sample before releasing a mould or tool.

=== A.3 Insertion Mechanics

[informative]
The 120° V-bevel produces a self-centring force on insertion.
As the lens is pressed into the rim:

. The ridge flanks contact the groove flanks and generate a lateral (radial) force component that centres the lens.
. The lens snaps into the groove once the ridge clears the groove entrance lips.

The snap action is primarily elastic deformation of the rim material.
For stiffer rim materials (titanium, thick acetate), the insertion technique should involve slightly bowing the lens into the rim starting from one side and walking the lens in incrementally.
For flexible rims (thin acetate, nylon), the rim can be gently spread apart and the lens pressed straight in.

=== A.4 Optical Centration and Pupillary Distance

[informative]
The UES standard does not specify optical centration.
Because lenses are standardised in size, the optician or lens laboratory can consistently calculate horizontal decentration relative to the lens geometric centre.
For UES-C-M (diameter 61 mm, bridge 19 mm), the geometric centre of each lens sits at a monocular PD of: (bridge/2) + (lens diameter/2) = 9.5 + 30.5 = 40.0 mm from the midline.

Manufacturers are encouraged to publish a centration chart for each product code listing the expected monocular PD for each standard bridge width.

=== A.5 Semi-Rimless and Three-Piece Frames

[informative]
Semi-rimless frames (where the lower half of the rim is not present) and three-piece designs (no rim, lens drilled for screws) are not directly compatible with the UES bevel interface and are outside the scope of this specification.
