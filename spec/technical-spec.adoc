--
This document defines the technical specification for the standardized lens and lens rim interface of the Universal Eyeglass Socket.
It covers lens shapes and sizes, and the bevel interface that joins lenses to compliant frames.
--

== Introduction

=== Scope

This specification defines:

* Standard circular lens sizes and bridge widths (UES-C-XS / UES-C-S / UES-C-M / UES-C-L)
* The bevel interface — the mechanical joint that allows any compliant lens to seat in any compliant frame rim

=== Normative References

* ISO 8624: Ophthalmic optics — Spectacle frames — Measuring system and terminology
* ISO 12870: Ophthalmic optics — Spectacle frames — Requirements and test methods

=== Terms and Definitions

Bevel ridge:: The V-shaped ridge running around the perimeter of a UES lens that engages the frame groove.
Bevel groove:: The matching V-shaped groove in the inner wall of the frame rim.
Bridge width:: The clear distance between the inner edges of the two lens rims, measured horizontally at the nasal bridge, per ISO 8624.
Nasal side:: The side of the lens closest to the nose.
Temporal side:: The side of the lens closest to the temple.

For circular lenses this nasal tip coincides with the horizontal centreline.

=== Shape

In the initial release, all standard sizes use a circular lens outline.
The lens diameter is measured at the outermost extent of the lens body (excluding the bevel ridge).

== Bevel Interface

The bevel interface is the single mechanical feature that makes lenses and frames interchangeable.
All compliant lenses and frames **must** implement this interface exactly.

=== Lens Bevel Ridge

A symmetrical V-shaped ridge runs continuously around the full perimeter of the lens edge.

[cols="2,1,3", options="header"]
|===
|Parameter
|Value
|Description

|Depth
|1.0 mm
|Distance from the flat lens perimeter surface to the apex of the ridge

|Width at base
|1.2 mm
|Opening width measured at the lens perimeter surface

|Included angle
|120°
|Symmetrical V-angle; each face is 60° from the lens axis

|Apex position (axial)
|1.5 mm
|Distance from the lens outer circumference edge to the apex, measured along the lens face

|Apex radius
|0.20 mm
|Rounding radius at the tip of the ridge (prevents stress concentration)
|===

The ridge flanks are planar and symmetric about the lens mid-plane.
The apex is rounded with a 0.20 mm radius tangent arc to both flanks.

=== Frame Rim Bevel Groove

The frame rim carries a matching internal groove on its inner (aperture) wall.
The groove is slightly larger than the lens ridge by a defined assembly clearance,
ensuring a consistent clearance fit that allows tool-free hand installation
while the V geometry self-centres the lens and the frame material provides retention.

[cols="2,1,1,2", options="header"]
|===
|Parameter
|Lens Ridge
|Groove (nominal)
|Notes

|Depth
|1.00 mm
|1.05 mm
|+0.05 mm clearance; groove cut deeper into rim body

|Opening width
|1.20 mm
|1.30 mm
|+0.05 mm per flank (total +0.10 mm)

|Included angle
|120°
|120°
|Flanks parallel to lens ridge flanks

|Apex radius
|0.20 mm
|0.20 mm
|Hard contact reference at apex; retained to aid self-centring

|Axial position
|lens mid-plane
|mid-depth of rim
|Groove centred on rim axial midpoint
|===

NOTE: The 0.05 mm per-flank clearance is the single governing
parameter.  All groove dimensions are derived from the lens ridge dimensions plus this value.

== Compliance

=== Lens Compliance

A lens is UES-compliant if it:

1. Has one of the standard diameters (51 / 56 / 61 / 67 mm) within the tolerance of <<tolerances>>
2. Carries a continuous bevel ridge meeting the geometry in <<lens-bevel-ridge>>
3. Can be fully seated in a compliant frame rim (functional test) without tools

=== Frame Rim Compliance

A frame rim is UES-compliant if it:

1. Carries a bevel groove meeting the geometry in <<frame-rim-bevel-groove>> continuously around the full aperture
2. Accepts and retains a compliant lens of the appropriate size without tools
3. Bridge width matches the declared size code within tolerance
