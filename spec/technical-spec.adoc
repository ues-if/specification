== Introduction

=== Purpose

This specification exists to enable a market in interchangeable prescription lenses.
When every compliant lens and every compliant frame rim implement this mechanical interface, a consumer can:

* own multiple frames in one size and swap lenses between them at home without tools;
* replace a broken frame without discarding functional lenses;
* order lenses online and receive them ready to self-install.

=== Scope

This specification normatively defines:

. The contour model - a UES aperture outline as a list of control points of a cubic Bézier curve, shared by both lens body and frame rim aperture.
. Size codes (XS through L) that scale the unit shape to the physical lens diameter, together with the bridge widths paired with each size.
. The bevel ridge on the lens edge and the matching bevel groove on the frame rim inner wall — the single mechanical feature that makes the standard work.
. Lens edge geometry constraints (bevel zone width, edge face perpendicularity in the bevel zone).
. Frame rim body geometry (rim channel width and depth, aperture shape).
. Material requirements for the bevel zone of both product types.
. Surface finish requirements in the bevel zone.
. Environmental and durability requirements (temperature, humidity, UV, insertion/removal cycle life).
. Dimensional tolerances on all normative dimensions.

NOTE: Conformance test procedures and compliance declaration requirements are defined in the companion link:compliance-test.adoc[UES Compliance Test Procedures] document.

The following are outside scope:

* Optical prescription values, coatings, tints, and UV absorption — these remain entirely at the discretion of the lens manufacturer.
* Frame materials, temple styles, nose pad design, hinge mechanisms, and all other frame geometry not at the rim aperture.

=== Terms and Definitions

Bevel groove:: The V-shaped groove present continuously around the full inner (aperture) wall of a UES-compliant frame rim; the female mating feature of the interface.

Bevel ridge:: The V-shaped ridge formed continuously around the full perimeter of the edge of a UES-compliant lens; the male mating feature of the interface.

Bevel zone:: A fixed-width axial band around the full perimeter of the lens edge, centred on the lens mid-plane, within which the bevel ridge is formed; the flat land areas on each side of the ridge base lie on a common cylinder. Also, the corresponding annular region of the frame rim inner wall that carries the bevel groove.
The bevel zone width is the same regardless of prescription-driven lens body thickness.

Bridge width:: The clear distance between the inner nasal edges of the two lens rims, measured horizontally at the datum plane defined in ISO 8624 §5.

Clearance fit:: An assembly condition in which the groove dimensions exceed the ridge dimensions by a defined amount, ensuring the lens can be inserted and removed by hand. In the UES interface, this applies to the V-groove/ridge pair only; the outer cylindrical surface of the lens edge is a sliding fit against the rim channel inner wall.

Sliding fit:: An assembly condition in which two mating cylindrical surfaces share the same nominal diameter, with tolerances set so that the parts can slide together without force but with negligible radial play. In the UES interface, the lens flat land outer surface slides against the rim channel inner wall, providing radial centring.

Compliant lens:: A lens that satisfies all normative requirements of this specification for the lens role.

Compliant frame rim:: A frame rim that satisfies all normative requirements of this specification for the frame rim role.

Datum plane:: The vertical plane parallel to the lens front surface, passing through the horizontal centreline of the lens, as defined in ISO 8624.

Fully seated:: The condition in which the bevel ridge apex contacts the bevel groove apex along the entire perimeter and the lens front surface is flush with the frame rim front face, within the tolerances of this specification.

Insertion force:: The maximum force required to press a compliant lens into a fully seated position in a compliant rim.

Nominal:: The target dimension to which tolerances apply.

Retention force:: The minimum force required to unseat a fully seated compliant lens from a compliant rim by applying a load perpendicular to the lens plane at the centre of the lens.

Rim channel:: The annular recess in the frame rim that receives the lens edge including the bevel ridge; the bevel groove is cut into the inner wall of this channel.

Size code:: A one or two letter identifier (e.g. "XS") of the lens diameter.

Shape code:: A one or two letter identifier (e.g. "C") of the lens contour shape.

Product code:: A designator of the form UES-TT-SS, where TT is a one- or two-letter shape-type identifier (e.g., C for Circular) and SS is a size code (e.g., XS, S, M, L), that uniquely identifies a lens shape and physical scale; two products are mechanically compatible if and only if they carry the same product code.

Control point:: One of the ordered points P₀, P₁, … that define a UES looped cubic Bézier contour; see <<_contour_model>>.

Unit shape:: An aperture contour expressed in normalized coordinates such that (a) the shape centroid is at the origin and (b) the maximum distance between any two points on the contour (the shape diameter) is exactly 1.

== System Overview

=== Role of the UES Interface

[informative]
The UES interface is the sole point of mechanical coupling between a lens and a frame rim.
It is intentionally defined as a single, simple V-bevel feature so that:

* lenses and rims can be manufactured by independent parties without coordination beyond this specification;
* installation requires no tools, no adhesive, and no fasteners;
* the interface self-centres the lens during installation by the geometry of the V-groove.

=== Product Types

This specification governs two parts of two product types:

*Lenses geometry and groove* — the bevel ridge on the lens edge, together with the normative requirements on lens edge geometry in the bevel zone.

*Frame rim geometry and groove* — the bevel groove on the frame rim inner wall, together with the normative requirements on rim geometry in the bevel zone and rim aperture.

=== Interchangeability Model

A compliant lens of product code UES-TT-SS SHALL mate with any compliant frame rim of the same product code.
A compliant lens of product code UES-TT-SS SHALL NOT be expected to mate with a frame rim of a different product code.

[[_contour_model]]
== Contour Model

=== Overview

All UES aperture shapes are defined as *looped cubic Bézier curves* in a normalized coordinate system.
This single contour governs both the lens body outline and the frame rim aperture opening.
This decouples shape geometry from physical size: the shape is specified once as a unit shape with a fixed normalized diameter of 1, and the size codes in <<_size_catalogue>> scale it by the aperture diameter to produce physical dimensions.

=== Coordinate System and Normalization

The coordinate system for unit shapes is:

* Origin at the area centroid of the aperture contour (the centroid of the region bounded by the looped cubic Bézier curve).
* X-axis: horizontal, positive toward the temporal (right) side.
* Y-axis: vertical, positive toward the superior (top) direction.

A *unit shape* is an aperture contour expressed in normalized coordinates such that:

* The area centroid of the contour lies at the origin; and
* The *shape diameter* - the maximum distance between any two points on the contour - is exactly 1.

Formally, for the unit-shape curve C(t), t ∈ [0, 1]:

  max_{t,s ∈ [0,1]} ‖C(t) - C(s)‖ = 1.

A shape code identifies a particular unit shape via an ordered list of control points in this normalized coordinate system.

.UES aperture contour coordinate system and normalization
image::diagrams/definition.drawio.png[UES aperture contour coordinate system and normalization,600,align="center"]

=== Looped Cubic Bézier Representation

A UES aperture contour is defined by an ordered list of M = 3N control points P₀, P₁, …, P_{M−1}, forming N cubic Bézier segments.

Segment i (i = 0 … N−1) is the cubic Bézier curve:

  Bᵢ(t) = (1−t)³ P_{3i}
        + 3(1−t)² t  P_{3i+1}
        + 3(1−t) t²  P_{3i+2}
        + t³         P_{(3i+3) mod M},   t ∈ [0, 1]

The control point sequence SHALL be ordered *counter-clockwise* when viewed from the front of the lens (positive-Y-superior convention).
The contour is closed.
The last segment (i = N−1) ends at P_{(3N) mod M} = P₀ — no repeated point is stored; the closure is implicit in the modular indexing.

[informative]
In a cubic Bézier segment, P_{3i} and P_{(3i+3) mod M} are the on-curve endpoints (the curve passes through them), and P_{3i+1} and P_{3i+2} are the off-curve handle points that shape the segment without being on the curve.

== Lens Edge Geometry

[[_lens_outline]]
=== Lens Edge Outline

The physical edge outline (without the ridge) of the lens is derived from the unit shape of the declared shape code (see <<shape-catalogue>>) and the lens diameter D of the declared size code (see <<size-table>>) as follows: 
multiply the x- and y-coordinates of every control point in the unit shape by D (in millimetres).

The lens body SHALL conform to this scaled outline within the tolerances defined in <<_dimensional_tolerances>>.

[[_bevel_zone_width]]
=== Bevel Zone Width

The lens edge SHALL present a fixed-width axial band — the bevel zone — of nominal axial width *3.00 mm* continuously around the full perimeter, centred on the lens mid-plane.
Within the bevel zone, the flat land areas on each side of the bevel ridge base SHALL lie on a common cylinder (constant radius from the lens axis).

The flat lands on each side of the 1.20 mm ridge base are each nominally 0.90 mm wide (= (3.00 − 1.20) / 2).

.Lens Bevel Ridge Cross-Section
image::diagrams/bevel-lens-side.drawio.png[Lens Bevel Ridge Cross-Section,600,align="center"]

=== Edge Face Perpendicularity

The outer cylindrical face of the lens edge (the surface on which the bevel ridge is formed) SHALL be perpendicular to the lens plane within 2°.

NOTE: [informative] This 2° limit is chosen such that bevel groove contact per <<_bevel_groove>> remains achievable under worst-case tolerance stack-up.

[[_bevel_ridge]]

A symmetrical V-shaped ridge SHALL run continuously around the full perimeter of the lens edge.

The bevel ridge protrudes 1.0 mm radially beyond the lens body edge, giving an overall maximum diameter of *d* + 2.0 mm including the ridge.

NOTE: The 120° included angle maximises self-centering force during insertion while allowing manual release without the ridge plastically deforming the groove.
Narrower angles increase retention but may require tools to release; wider angles reduce retention below the minimum defined in <<_retention_force>>.

== Frame Rim Geometry

[[_rim_aperture_outline]]
=== Rim Aperture Outline

The shape of the rim aperture opening SHALL conform to the scaled contour of the declared product code: the same unit shape (see <<shape-catalogue>>) scaled by the lens diameter D of the declared size code (see <<size-table>>).

The rim aperture diameter is measured at the entrance plane of the rim channel, before the groove.
The rim aperture SHALL conform to this outline within the tolerances defined in <<_dimensional_tolerances>>.

[[_rim_channel_width]]
=== Rim Channel Width

The inner wall of the frame rim aperture SHALL present a fixed-width axial channel — the rim channel — of nominal axial width *3.20 mm* continuously around the full aperture perimeter, centred on the rim mid-plane.
Within the rim channel, the flat land areas on each side of the bevel groove opening SHALL lie on a common cylinder (constant radius from the rim axis).

The flat lands on each side of the 1.30 mm groove opening are each nominally 0.95 mm wide (= (3.20 − 1.30) / 2).

NOTE: The 3.20 mm channel width equals the lens bevel zone width (3.00 mm) plus 0.10 mm axial clearance per side, and is independent of lens prescription.

.Frame Channel Cross-Section
image::diagrams/bevel-frame-side.drawio.png[Frame Channel Cross-Section,600,align="center"]

=== Inner Wall Perpendicularity

The inner wall of the rim channel (the surface on which the bevel groove is formed) SHALL be perpendicular to the rim plane within 2°.

NOTE: [informative] This 2° limit is chosen such that bevel groove contact per <<_bevel_groove>> remains achievable under worst-case tolerance stack-up.

NOTE: The 0.05 mm per-flank clearance is the single governing assembly parameter.
All groove dimensions are derived from the lens ridge dimensions plus this value.
Do not independently tighten groove tolerances beyond those in <<_dimensional_tolerances>> without validating against the full tolerance stack.

The 0.05 mm per-flank clearance produces a nominal air gap that:

* allows hand insertion of a cold lens into a cold rim (no thermal interference);
* accommodates thermal expansion from -20 °C to +60 °C for common lens and rim materials;
* provides predictable contact force along both flanks simultaneously (the V geometry centres the lens axially).

=== Assembly Clearance and Fit

The UES interface uses two distinct fit types acting simultaneously:

* *Sliding fit* — the flat land areas on the lens edge (at the nominal lens body diameter d) bear against the inner wall of the rim channel. This is the radial centring mechanism: the lens is located in the aperture by this cylindrical contact, not by the V-groove.
* *Clearance fit* — the V-ridge on the lens sits inside the V-groove on the rim with a defined flank clearance. This is the axial retention mechanism: the flanks resist any force pushing the lens out of the plane.

The lens ridge SHALL NOT be a press fit (interference fit) in the groove.

=== Frame Rim Face Flatness

The front face of the frame rim (the surface visible from the front) SHALL be planar to within 0.30 mm over the aperture diameter.
This planarity ensures that a fully seated lens front face is flush with the rim front face.

[[_materials]]
== Material Requirements

=== Lens Edge Bevel Zone Material

The material forming the bevel ridge SHALL:

* Have a Shore D hardness ≥ 70 (or equivalent for the material class) to prevent permanent deformation of the ridge under insertion force.
* Have a Charpy unnotched impact strength ≥ 5 kJ/m² (ISO 179-1) so the ridge does not fracture during insertion or removal at temperatures down to -20 °C.
* Not exhibit cold flow (creep) that reduces bevel ridge depth by more than 0.05 mm after 1,000 hours at 60 °C under a lateral force of 5 N.

NOTE: Common lens materials that satisfy these requirements include CR-39 (allyl diglycol carbonate), polycarbonate (PC), Trivex, and high-index glass.

=== Frame Rim Bevel Groove Material

The material forming the bevel groove SHALL:

* Have a Shore D hardness ≥ 60 (lower than lens to place incremental wear preferentially on the rim, which is the less costly element to replace).
* Have sufficient elasticity to allow hand flexing of the rim sufficient for manual lens installation and removal.
* Not exhibit cold flow that narrows the groove opening width by more than 0.05 mm after 1,000 hours at 60 °C under a lateral force of 5 N.

NOTE: Common frame rim materials that satisfy these requirements include acetate (cellulose acetate), nylon (PA), nylon-glass fibre composite, titanium alloy (for metal rims with grooved wire), and spring steel wire.

=== Prohibited Materials and Treatments

The following SHALL NOT be applied within the bevel zone on either the lens ridge or the groove:

* Soft elastomeric coatings with Shore A hardness < 50.
* Lubricants containing solvents that attack polycarbonate (ketones, esters, chlorinated solvents).
* Adhesives or bonding agents (the interface is mechanical only).

== Environmental and Durability Requirements

=== Temperature

==== Operating Temperature Range

A fully mated lens-and-rim assembly SHALL meet all retention force requirements of <<_retention_force>> throughout the operating temperature range of -20 °C to +60 °C.

==== Storage Temperature Range

A lens or frame rim, un-mated, SHALL survive storage at -30 °C to +80 °C for up to 72 hours with no dimensional change exceeding 0.05 mm on any normative bevel dimension, measured after return to 23 °C ± 2 °C.

==== Thermal Shock

A mated assembly SHALL survive five cycles of:

. Immersion in water at 5 °C ± 2 °C for 30 minutes.
. Transfer within 30 seconds to water at 55 °C ± 2 °C for 30 minutes.

After completion, the assembly SHALL meet the minimum retention force of <<_retention_force>> and the lens SHALL show no visible cracks under 1,000 lux illumination.

=== Humidity

A mated assembly SHALL survive 240 hours at 40 °C and 95% relative humidity (non-condensing) with no loss of retention force below the minimum defined in <<_retention_force>> and no visible corrosion, delamination, or warping that would prevent re-seating.

=== Ultraviolet Exposure

A lens and a frame rim SHALL each survive 200 hours of UV exposure per ISO 4892-3 (fluorescent UV lamps, UV-A 340 nm, irradiance 0.76 W/m²·nm) with no:

* Loss of bevel ridge depth exceeding 0.05 mm.
* Loss of groove opening width exceeding 0.05 mm.
* Cracking or crazing of any bevel zone surface.

=== Insertion and Removal Cycle Life

The bevel interface SHALL be rated for a minimum of 500 insertion and removal cycles (one cycle = full installation to fully seated + full removal).

After 500 cycles, the assembly SHALL:

. Pass the retention force test.
. Show no bevel ridge depth reduction exceeding 0.10 mm.
. Show no groove opening width increase exceeding 0.10 mm.
. Allow the lens to be installed and removed by hand without tools.

NOTE: 500 cycles is the baseline.
A manufacturer MAY claim a higher cycle rating (e.g., "UES 2000-cycle rated") if the product passes the endurance test at the higher cycle count.

=== Drop and Impact (Informative)

[informative]
UES-compliant lenses and rims are not required to pass a drop or ballistic impact test as part of this specification; such requirements are governed by national eyewear safety standards (e.g., EN ISO 16321, ANSI Z87.1).
Manufacturers are encouraged to test their products to the applicable safety standard independently of UES compliance.

== Mechanical Performance Requirements

[[_insertion_force]]
=== Insertion Force

Insertion force is the force required to press a compliant lens into a fully seated position in a compliant rim.

A compliant lens of any product code SHALL seat fully in a compliant rim of the same product code by applying a force no greater than:

[cols="1,1", options="header"]
|===
|Product Code
|Maximum Insertion Force

|UES-C-XS
|12 N

|UES-C-S
|14 N

|UES-C-M
|16 N

|UES-C-L
|18 N
|===

NOTE: Forces scale with perimeter length.
The limits above correspond to approximately 75 N/m of bevel perimeter.
The insertion force test procedure is defined in the link:compliance-test.adoc[UES Compliance Test Procedures].

[[_retention_force]]
=== Retention Force

Retention force is the minimum force required to unseat a fully seated compliant lens by applying a centralised normal load perpendicular to the lens plane.

A fully seated compliant lens SHALL require a force no less than the values below to unseat, measured per the link:compliance-test.adoc[UES Compliance Test Procedures]:

[cols="1,1", options="header"]
|===
|Product Code
|Minimum Retention Force

|UES-C-XS
|20 N

|UES-C-S
|22 N

|UES-C-M
|25 N

|UES-C-L
|28 N
|===

NOTE: The minimum retention force is set high enough that a lens will not unseat when the frame is dropped face-down from 1 m, but low enough to be overcome by hand during intentional removal.

=== Seating Flush Requirement

When fully seated, the lens front face SHALL be flush with the frame rim front face within ± 0.30 mm across the full rim perimeter.
A lens recessed more than 0.30 mm or proud more than 0.30 mm of the rim front face is non-conforming.
