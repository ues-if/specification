Pilot business model for **Reframe Kids**, a UES-compliant kids' frames brand, to validate consumer demand for universal interchangeable lenses.

== 1. Context and Hypothesis

=== 1.1 Strategic Role of Kids Segment

The global kids eyewear segment is a multi-tens-of-billions market with high growth (around 10% CAGR through 2030), and Europe is already the largest revenue region.
Children break and outgrow frames more often than adults, which amplifies the pain of replacing complete pairs when only frames or only lenses are the problem.
Parents also already face frequent replacement cycles, and some reimbursement schemes (e.g. UK NHS vouchers) explicitly recognise the high rate of loss/damage in children's glasses.

*Hypothesis*: **Reframe Kids**, a UES-compliant kids frame brand that separates lenses from frames and makes frame replacement cheap, fast, and fun, will:
- Lower total cost of ownership for parents over 2-3 years.
- Make breakage and style changes much less painful.
- Demonstrate clear consumer pull that can later convince large lens and frame players.

=== 1.2 Positioning within UES

This kids frames pilot is:
- The **first commercial implementation** of UES on the frame side.
- Optimised for **durability + low cost + style rotation**, not luxury fashion.
- Designed to be **copyable by others**: success is measured by standard adoption, not moat strength.

The company operates as a for-profit that:
- Produces frames through a partner manufacturer, using the open UES specification.
- Sells the frames on optical retail channels and through school partnerships.

== 2. Value Proposition (Kids / Parents)

=== 2.1 Problems for Parents and Kids

For children 5-10, current prescription glasses have recurring issues:
- Frames break frequently during play and sports.
- Kids' tastes change (colour, style, characters), forcing frame+lens replacement.
- Prescriptions may change while frames are still fine, or vice-versa.
- Replacement logistics are slow and often require a full visit; some warranties cover only limited damage or only a single replacement.

=== 2.2 UES-based Solution

We launch Reframe Kids, frames with:
- Standard UES lens interface (UES-C-XS and UES-C-S) for children between 5 and 10 years old
- Snap-in lenses: parents can install lenses at home in under a minute with clear visual confirmation.

Benefits for parents:
- When frames break, they buy only a low-cost frame; lenses are reused.
- The child can have 2-3 frames in different colours/styles for the same lenses.
- If prescription changes, only lenses change; existing frames remain valid.
- Potential to pre-buy spare frames for peace of mind (school, grandparents' house, sports kit bag).

Benefits for kids:
- Less drama when glasses break - backup frame already at home at low cost.
- Ability to change style or colour without needing new lenses.
- If glasses break at school, the child can swap to a spare frame right there and then, without waiting for a parent or an optician appointment.

== 3. Business Model

=== 3.1 Target Customer and Persona

Primary customer: **Parent of a child 5-10 with a stable or slowly changing prescription** sensitive to:
- Cost of frequent replacements.
- Hassle of repeated optician visits.
- Child's emotional attachment to specific frames/looks.

Early geographic focus for pilot:
- **Portugal (Amadora)** as the launch market and pilot region.

=== 3.2 Offering and Revenue Streams

Reframe Kids sells frames:

- Product: UES-compliant kids frames (2 sizes, multiple colours, some themed drops).
- Price point: target retail 25-40 EUR per frame.
- Key feature: "lens-compatible forever" as long as UES standard is maintained.

=== 3.3 Cost Structure

Main cost drivers:
- Design and tooling for UES-compliant child frames (2 base sizes).
- Manufacturing (plastic injection / 3D printing for early runs, then scaled).
- Marketing, especially parent trust building and optometrist outreach.
- UES participation: annual Member or Logo Licensee fee (modest vs total budget).

=== 3.4 Go-to-Market Options

We consider two complementary channels:

**Optometrist / clinic partners**
   - Provide physical fitting and professional trust.
   - Sell our frames with UES message: "If these frames break, just replace the frame; lenses stay."
   - Spread on frame sales.

**School partnerships**
   - Donate a stock of UES-compliant spare frames (in the two pilot sizes) to participating schools.
   - Schools keep the frames in the nurse's office or administration desk.
   - When a child breaks their glasses during the school day, staff snap the existing lenses into a spare frame immediately — the child is back to full vision in minutes, without waiting for a parent or a same-day optician visit.
   - Promoted to parents as a safety and convenience benefit: "Reframe Kids frames are available at your child's school. Breakage no longer means a lost school day."
   - Frames given to schools act as a marketing touchpoint: parents who see the swap happen first-hand become the strongest word-of-mouth advocates.

== 4. Pilot Design

=== 4.1 Objectives and Metrics

The pilot's main objective is *hypothesis validation*, not short-term profit.

Key questions:
- can frame manufacturers produce UES frame with the specification alone?
- can lens manufacturers produce UES lenses that fit securely in the frames?
- Will parents understand and value lens-frame decoupling?
- Can 2 sizes cover the majority of 5-10 year olds?
