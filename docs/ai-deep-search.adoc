= Publicly Available Human Interpupillary Distance (IPD) Datasets and Studies
:toc:
:toclevels: 3
:sectnums:

== Executive Summary

I found a small but useful public evidence base for measured interpupillary distance (IPD / PD) in human populations.

The strongest sources are concentrated in four clusters:

* Military / ergonomic anthropometry
* Respirator-fit and occupational head-and-face surveys
* Clinical / ophthalmic normative studies
* A small number of reusable open datasets

The most reusable machine-readable resources I could verify are:

* ANSUR II public release
* Taiwan PLOS One respirator-fit supplementary dataset

Most other sources are either article-level summary statistics or projects whose data are only partially open, commercial, or tool-mediated rather than directly downloadable.

Across the catalogue, adult male means usually sit in the mid-60 mm range and adult female means in the low-60 mm range. However, differences are affected by:

* Sampling frame
* Ethnicity
* Age
* Measurement method
* Far vs near IPD
* Binocular vs monocular PD

Small cross-study differences should not be over-interpreted unless the measurement method and recruitment frame are comparable.

Coverage is best for adults in:

* United States
* China
* Malaysia
* Iran
* Turkey
* Pakistan
* South Africa

Coverage is weaker for:

* Latin America
* Oceania
* Europe outside selected optometric / anthropometric datasets
* Children under 5
* Public raw datasets with direct IPD variables

A methodological point matters: some sources report far binocular IPD, some report near and far IPD, some distinguish binocular vs monocular PD, and anthropometric surveys sometimes label the same construct "interpupillary breadth."

In this document, "interpupillary breadth" is treated as equivalent to binocular IPD only where the source explicitly defines it as the distance between the two pupils.

== Search Method

Sources searched included:

* PubMed / PubMed Central
* Government anthropometry repositories
* Open-access journal sites
* Occupational health and respirator-fit studies
* Anthropometric project pages
* 3D face / headform datasets

Search terms included:

* interpupillary distance
* inter-pupillary distance
* pupillary distance
* interpupillary breadth
* pupilometer / pupillometer
* autorefractor / autorefractometer
* anthropometric survey
* head and face anthropometry
* respirator fit
* workers
* children
* craniofacial anthropometry

Inclusion criteria:

* Human participants
* Directly measured IPD / PD, or explicit IPD variable
* Clear enough metadata to identify population, sample size, and measurement method

Excluded:

* Studies reporting only inner canthal distance
* Studies reporting only outer canthal distance
* Sources where IPD had to be inferred without clear pupil landmarks
* Papers with insufficient accessible metadata

== Measurement Methods and Comparability

IPD values are not directly interchangeable across all studies.

Important distinctions:

* Far IPD vs near IPD
* Binocular IPD vs monocular PD
* Anatomical pupil center vs corneal reflex
* Manual ruler vs pupillometer vs autorefractometer
* Direct anthropometry vs 2D photogrammetry vs 3D scan landmarks

[cols="1,2,2,2", options="header"]
|===
|Method
|Strengths
|Cautions
|Expected Use

|Digital pupillometer
|Purpose-built, fast, repeatable
|Device family matters
|Best field benchmark

|Autorefractometer
|Standard clinical workflow, scalable
|Usually distance IPD only; often clinic-based
|Clinical norms

|Manual ruler / Viktorin method
|Cheap, universal

|Landmarking protocol matters
|Design and headform generation
|===

== Practical Interpretation

For open adult microdata:

* Start with ANSUR II.

For open participant-level Asian face-fit data:

* Use the Taiwan PLOS One supplementary dataset.

For national or semi-national adult estimates:

* Malaysia NHMS-based study is especially useful.

For pediatric IPD:

* Chinese children study is large, but not raw-data accessible.

For 3D product design:

* SizeChina, CAESAR, DINED, and FaceBase-type resources may be useful, but access and licensing vary.

== Recommended Workflow

If you need measured IPD distributions for software, eyewear, VR, or synthetic face generation:

. Download ANSUR II.
. Extract the interpupillary breadth column.
. Convert units to mm if necessary.
. Separate by sex and any demographic fields available.
. Fit distributions:
.. Gaussian
.. KDE
.. Gaussian mixture model
. Validate means against external studies:
.. U.S. respirator users
.. Chinese workers
.. Malaysia NHMS
.. Iran / Turkey / Pakistan clinical studies
. For children, use literature-derived age curves rather than adult datasets.
. Do not mix near and far IPD without correction.

== Terminology

=== IPD

Interpupillary distance. Distance between the centers of the pupils.

=== PD

Pupillary distance. Often used clinically, especially in optics.

=== Binocular PD

Total distance between the two pupils.

=== Monocular PD

Distance from nose bridge / facial midline to one pupil.

=== Near IPD

IPD measured for near fixation. Usually smaller than distance IPD because of convergence.

=== Distance / Far IPD

IPD measured for far fixation.

=== Interpupillary Breadth

Anthropometric term often equivalent to binocular IPD when explicitly defined as distance between pupils.

=== Inner Canthal Distance

Distance between inner eye corners. Not equivalent to IPD.

=== Outer Canthal Distance

Distance between outer eye corners. Not equivalent to IPD.

== Final Notes

The open-data shortlist is short:

* ANSUR II
* Taiwan PLOS One supplementary dataset

Most other useful sources are summary-statistic studies. They are still valuable, especially for checking whether a synthetic or design distribution is plausible across population groups.

For design work, the safest approach is to store source-specific metadata and never treat all IPD values as one interchangeable global measurement.

== Newly Identified Dataset Categories

The web search revealed several broader categories of resources beyond traditional anthropometric surveys.

=== Category 1: Direct Anthropometric IPD Datasets

These contain explicit measured IPD variables.

Best examples:

* ANSUR II
* Taiwan respirator dataset
* Chinese worker survey
* Malaysia NHMS study

Advantages:

* Direct measurements
* Easier statistical analysis
* Reliable population distributions

Disadvantages:

* Fewer publicly downloadable datasets
* Often limited demographics

=== Category 2: 3D Craniofacial Morphology Repositories

These contain:

* 3D meshes
* Landmarks
* Facial geometry

Best examples:

* FaceBase
* SizeChina

Advantages:

* Extremely rich geometric data
* Allows arbitrary derived measurements
* Better for realistic human modeling

Disadvantages:

* Access restrictions
* Large files
* More processing required

=== Category 3: Eye-Tracking / VR Eye Datasets

These focus on:

* Eye geometry
* Pupil tracking
* Gaze estimation

Best examples:

* OpenEDS
* TEyeD

Advantages:

* Massive eye-region image volumes
* Useful for eye tracking and VR

Disadvantages:

* Weak demographic coverage
* IPD usually indirect
* Requires reconstruction pipelines

== Important Observation

The strongest newly discovered resource is clearly FaceBase.

Why:

* Large-scale craniofacial repository
* Public summaries
* Individual-level controlled-access data
* Landmark coordinates
* 3D meshes
* Anthropometric variables
* Research-grade metadata

FaceBase is likely the closest thing to a modern reusable craniofacial "ground truth" ecosystem for IPD-adjacent measurements currently available publicly.

== Important Caveat

Many repositories do NOT expose a variable literally named:

* interpupillary_distance

Instead, IPD may need to be computed from:

* Pupil landmarks
* Eye center coordinates
* 3D landmark geometry
* Inter-landmark Euclidean distances

Therefore, many modern datasets are:

* geometry-first
rather than
* scalar-measurement-first

This is especially true for:

* 3D stereophotogrammetry datasets
* Mesh repositories
* Computer vision datasets
* VR eye-tracking datasets
