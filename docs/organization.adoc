This document describes the organizational structure, governance model, and operations of the Universal Eyeglass Standards Consortium (UESC), the entity responsible for developing and maintaining the Universal Eyeglass Standard specification.
--

== Mission and Vision

=== Mission Statement

To develop, maintain, and promote an open, royalty-free standard for interchangeable prescription eyeglass lenses that:

* Reduces consumer costs and increases choice
* Minimizes waste in the eyewear industry
* Ensures optical quality and safety
* Enables innovation in eyewear design

=== Vision

A world where prescription lenses are portable across frames, where broken glasses no longer mean replacing expensive prescription lenses, and where consumers have unlimited frame choices without sacrificing optical quality.

=== Values

* **Openness**: Standards development is transparent and inclusive
* **Independence**: Decisions serve users and industry collectively, not individual companies
* **Excellence**: Technical rigor and safety are never compromised
* **Sustainability**: Design choices minimize environmental impact
* **Accessibility**: Standards enable affordable, quality eyewear for all

== Organizational Structure

=== Legal Entity

**Proposed Structure**: Non-profit association or consortium

**Jurisdiction**: To be determined based on founding members
* Options: USA (501(c)(6) trade association), EU (AISBL), Switzerland (Association)

**Rationale for Non-Profit**:
* Ensures independence from commercial interests
* Facilitates open, royalty-free licensing
* Enables grant funding and foundation support
* Builds trust with consumers and regulators

=== Governance Bodies

==== Board of Directors

**Composition** (7-11 members):

* 3-5 Industry representatives (manufacturers, retailers)
* 2-3 Professional representatives (optometrists, optical engineers)
* 1-2 Consumer representatives
* 1-2 Independent technical experts

**Term**: 3 years, staggered terms, maximum 2 consecutive terms

**Responsibilities**:
* Set strategic direction
* Approve annual budget and business plan
* Appoint and oversee Executive Director
* Approve major partnerships and initiatives
* Ensure financial sustainability

**Voting**: Simple majority for standard decisions, 2/3 supermajority for:
* Specification major version changes
* Changes to licensing terms
* Amendments to bylaws

==== Technical Committee

**Composition** (9-15 members):

* Optical engineers (3-4)
* Lens manufacturers (2-3)
* Frame manufacturers (2-3)
* Materials scientists (1-2)
* Safety/regulatory experts (1-2)
* Academic researchers (1-2)

**Term**: 2 years, renewable

**Responsibilities**:
* Develop and maintain technical specification
* Review proposed specification changes
* Establish testing procedures
* Evaluate new materials and technologies
* Issue technical guidance documents
* Approve reference implementations

**Voting**: Consensus-based, with 2/3 vote fallback. Any member can escalate controversial technical decisions to Board.

==== Certification Board

**Composition** (5-7 members):

* Quality assurance experts (2)
* Testing laboratory representatives (2)
* Industry compliance specialists (2)
* Legal/IP expert (1)

**Term**: 2 years, renewable

**Responsibilities**:
* Develop and maintain certification criteria
* Accredit testing laboratories
* Review certification applications
* Handle compliance disputes
* Maintain list of certified products and manufacturers
* Issue and revoke certifications

**Voting**: Simple majority

==== Working Groups

Task-focused groups for specific activities:

* **Patents & IP Working Group**: Patent landscape, freedom-to-operate, defensive publications
* **Marketing & Communications Working Group**: Brand management, consumer education
* **Manufacturing Working Group**: Production best practices, supply chain
* **Sustainability Working Group**: Environmental impact, circular economy metrics
* **International Expansion Working Group**: Regional adaptations, regulatory harmonization

Working groups report to relevant governance body (Technical Committee or Board).

Benefits:

Universities and research institutions studying eyewear, optics, or related fields.

Benefits:

Optometrists, optical engineers, and industry professionals.

Benefits:
- Newsletter and updates
- Attend member events
- Participate in public comment periods

Annual dues: €100-250

**Rights**:
* Access to all published specifications and technical documents

**Obligations**:

== Intellectual Property Policy

=== Specification Licensing

The Universal Eyeglass Standard specification is published under a **royalty-free, perpetual, worldwide license** that allows any party to:

* Implement the specification in products
* Create derivative specifications (with attribution)
* Use the specification for certification testing
* Distribute and reproduce the specification

**Restrictions**:
* May not claim exclusive rights to the standard
* May not restrict others' implementation
* Derivative specifications must not be named "Universal Eyeglass Standard" without authorization

=== Patent Policy

==== Defensive Publication Strategy

SGC commits to publishing detailed technical specifications to create prior art, preventing future patenting of core standard concepts by third parties.

==== Patent Non-Assertion Covenant

Founding members and contributors agree:

1. **Royalty-Free License**: Grant all implementers a royalty-free license to any patents necessary to implement the specification
2. **Non-Assertion**: Will not assert patent claims against any party implementing the specification in good faith
3. **Defensive Termination**: License terminates for any party that asserts patents against SGC or compliant implementers

==== Patent Review Process

Before publishing specification or major amendments:

1. Technical Committee identifies potentially relevant patents
2. Patents & IP Working Group conducts freedom-to-operate analysis
3. If potential conflicts identified:
   * Seek design-around solutions, OR
   * Negotiate licenses from patent holders, OR
   * Publish alternative approaches as optional methods

=== Certification Marks

**"UES Certified" Mark**: Registered trademark of UESC

* Awarded to products that pass certification testing
* Licensed to manufacturers under certification agreement
* Revocable for non-compliance
* Licensed at cost-recovery rates

**"Universal Eyeglass Standard" Brand**: Owned by UESC

* Generic term for the standard itself (not trademarkable)
* "Universal Eyeglass Standards Consortium" and "UESC" are protected
* Members may state "complies with Universal Eyeglass Standard specification" without license

== Certification Program

=== Overview

The UESC certification program ensures that products labeled as "UES Certified" meet the technical requirements of the specification.

=== Certification Process

1. **Application**: Manufacturer submits application with product details
2. **Testing**: Product sent to accredited testing laboratory
3. **Review**: Certification Board reviews test reports
4. **Approval**: Certificate issued (valid 3 years)
5. **Surveillance**: Random market sampling for ongoing compliance
6. **Renewal**: Recertification required every 3 years

=== Testing Laboratories

==== Accreditation

SGC accredits independent testing laboratories based on:

* ISO/IEC 17025 accreditation (or equivalent)
* Appropriate equipment and expertise
* No conflict of interest with manufacturers
* Agreement to follow SGC testing procedures

==== Accredited Labs (Current)

_To be established upon program launch_

Target initial accreditations:

* Colts Laboratories (USA)

=== Compliance and Enforcement

==== Non-Compliance Actions

If certified product fails surveillance testing or complaint investigation:

1. **Notice**: Manufacturer notified of specific non-compliance
2. **Corrective Action**: 60-90 days to remedy and retest
3. **Suspension**: Certification suspended if not remedied
4. **Revocation**: Persistent or severe non-compliance → permanent revocation
5. **Public Notice**: Revocations published on SGC website

==== Appeals Process

Manufacturers may appeal certification decisions:

* Appeal to Certification Board within 30 days
* Board reviews within 60 days
* Final appeal to Board of Directors
* Independent arbitration if dispute persists

==== Unauthorized Use

UESC may pursue legal action against:

* Use of "UES Certified" mark without valid certification
* False claims of compliance with Universal Eyeglass Standard specification
* Misuse of UESC trademarks

=== Revenue Sources

1. **Membership Dues** (target: 50-60% of revenue)
   * Based on member category and company size
   * Predictable, recurring revenue

2. **Certification Fees** (target: 30-40% of revenue)
   * Application and testing coordination fees
   * Annual certification mark licensing

3. **Grants and Donations** (target: 5-10% of revenue)
   * Foundation grants for sustainability impact
   * Government innovation grants
   * Industry donations

4. **Sponsorships** (target: 5% of revenue)
   * Event sponsorships
   * Research partnerships
   * Educational programs

== Conclusion

The Standard Glasses Consortium is structured as an independent, open organization serving the collective interests of consumers, manufacturers, and professionals. Through transparent governance, rigorous technical standards, and inclusive membership, SGC aims to transform the eyewear industry toward greater choice, lower cost, and sustainability.
